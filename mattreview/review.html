Persona 5 Review

A wonderful advancement on the formula used by its predecessors, and overall, an amazing game.

It's Paprika, yo!

Persona 5 is a critically acclaimed and widely praised 2016 Japanese Role-Playing Game. It's a long game that starts slow, but when it picks up, it doesn't slow down.

Persona 5 starts you in an intense intro sequence, placing you and your team inside of a casino, with your goal being to get out of the casino. This intro sequence is good for the player, as it introduces mechanics that will appear later on, such as hiding, ambushing enemies, and the way battles work. As mentioned, part of this intro sequence is used as an oppurtunity to introduce the player to the combat system. This is where Persona 5 shows its improvements on the forumlas provided by 2 of its predecessors, Persona 4 and Persona 3.

Persona 5's battle system has been simplified, with every option now being only one button press. This is much more convinient than Persona 3 and 4's battle options, those being a wheel you would have to move through to reach the option you wanted. This is also very innovative for the series as it makes very good use of the buttons, having the options on a good portion of them.

Persona 5 puts you into the shoes of a silent protagonist. After getting himself into a problem, he is forced to move to another place and live with a coffee shop owner. Being in a new place also forces the protagonist to go to a new school. It's in this school where everything changes. After making some friends, you and a new friend find yourself in a new world. The metaverse, the new world, is a new take on the second worlds present in the Persona series. Persona 3's Dark Hour presented a seemingly endless tower to explore, and Persona 4's Midnight Chanenel provided explorable dungeons that would move the story along. It's Persona 4's Midnight Chanenel that Persona 5 improves on.

Persona 5's Palaces provide larger dungeon enviroments to explore, and many puzzles inside of them. These palaces also move the story along, being vital important to the story and all having some important story moments inside of them.
